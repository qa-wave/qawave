## Copy Notes for Web Designer

1. **Hero badge** ("Agentic AI for QA Engineering") sits above the headline as a small label -- establishes category before the reader hits the main line.
2. **Stat boxes** in Problem Statement should be visually heavy on the number, with label and supporting text secondary. The numbers do the emotional work.
3. **UVP pillar cards** should be scannable at headline + tagline level. Description and bullets are for readers who engage deeper.
4. **How It Works** steps should read left-to-right (desktop) or top-to-bottom (mobile) with clear numbered progression. Keep it tight -- this section is about demystification, not depth.
5. **Testimonial** is from a pilot program (product is pre-GA). Attribution is deliberately anonymized -- replace with named attribution when available.
6. **Exit strategy line** in Tech Credibility section is a trust signal aimed directly at the persona's "vendor lock-in" concern. Give it visual weight -- it is not throwaway copy.
7. **CTAs** are consistent throughout: "Start Free Pilot" (primary, action-oriented, technical) and "Watch Technical Demo" (secondary, lower commitment). No "Book a Demo" or "Talk to Sales" -- the audience distrusts sales-led funnels.
8. **Tone check**: zero instances of "revolutionary," "cutting-edge," "next-generation," "game-changing," or "transform." Every claim maps to a specific capability described in the strategy document.
